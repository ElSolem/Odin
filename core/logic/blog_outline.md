Blog Outline Template: The Seven W’s + H
1. Who

Define the actors involved.

Identify audiences, stakeholders, antagonists, or conceptual “characters.”

Clarify whose perspective anchors the piece.

2. What

Name the core idea, problem, claim, or phenomenon.

State what is being examined or challenged.

Give the reader the conceptual object they will be wrestling with.

3. When

Establish temporal context.

Past origins, current relevance, and future trajectory.

Situate the idea in time so evolution becomes visible.

4. Where

Describe the environment or domain in which the idea lives.

Technical landscape, cultural context, metaphysical terrain—whatever fits the topic.

The “location” of meaning.

5. Why

Present the underlying motivation.

Explain the tension, contradiction, or misconception that gives the topic weight.

Clarify why the reader should care.

6. How

Lay out mechanisms, reasoning, processes, or demonstrations.

Show the inner workings: examples, evidence, derivations, experiments.

This is where the gears turn.

7. Way

Describe the path forward.

Practical implications, new frameworks, philosophical takeaways, recommended actions.

This section is the “vector” pointing out of the article.

(Integrate if the article needs more space to breathe.)

Summary

A succinct wrap-up reinforcing the argument.

References or Inspirations
